Overview

I made a Galileo Thermometer, which is based on the principle that the density of a liquid changes with temperature. As the liquid in the thermometer (in my case water) reaches steady started with the surrounding temperature, the vials in the liquid, which stay at a constant mass, and thus constant density, will either sink or float. By looking at the lowest floating vial and highest sinking vial, we can determine that the temperature of the liquid and thus the surrounding environment, is between the two temperature marked on the vials.

At first, I tried to make a model to determine the masses of the vials. However, I found out that those masses did not actually work in practice, whether because my thermometer or scale was not precise or accurate enough, or because of another factor. Thus, I ended up measuring the masses of the vials by hand, adding and removing liquid from the vials until they just floated or sank at the target temperatures.
