Overview

For this project, I worked with three other people to try and restore a kayak from 2012 and make it autonomous. The goal was to create another vehicle for Olin's Climate Robotics and Expeditonary Science Technology (CREST) research lab to use on the water. The kayak would be the largest vehicle in the fleet, useful for carrying larger payloads, including a CTD (conductivity, temperature, and depth instruments) and a DGEU (Greenhouse gas analyzer), and travel longer in choppier waters.


As one of my first big projects, I learned a lot about control systems and power distribution. I learned about analog vs digital signals, how to have sensors communicate with a computer and flight controller, and how to have that all in turn communicate with a ground station. I also learned how important documentation is, especially because we inherited a poorly documented project.

At the end of the semester, we had finished deciding what components we needed, and creating a system diagram for wires and communication protocols. I also started to set up our pixhawk with ArduPilot and get familiar with the software. However we ultimately decided not to continue as the kayak only had a single motor, while would need its own custom airframe.
